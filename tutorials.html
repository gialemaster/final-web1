Beginner Tutorials

This page is all about the very basics of knitting, from stitches to how to read a pattern.

The Basics

The longtail cast-on

The longtail cast-on is a very versatile cast on technique, but it requires a bit of guessing. It makes a very stretchy foundation, but you need a long tail of yarn. The more stitches, the longer it must be.

Cable cast-on

The cable cast-on is the opposite of the long tail cast-on. It is very sturdy and can be ideal when a stiff edge is needed.

The knit stitch

The knit stitch is the most basic stitch, and therefore the most important. It is used in Stocking stitch (sometimes called stockinette>and is the most commonly used stitch in knitting.

The purl stitch

The second most common knit stitch, the purl (pronounced "pearl") is the second technique used in Stocking stitch.

Basic cast-off

The basic bind off is the cast-off equivalent of the cable cast-on. It's very sturdy and creates a nice clean edge.

Stretchy cast-off

Like the basic cast-off, the stretchy cast off is the counterpart of the longtail cast-on. It's great for things like socks or sweater neck holes.

How to use Ravelry.com

Ravelry.com is a massive pattern database and community, it is the best option for any knitter in search of a project.

How to Search Ravelry

Ravelry is a vast website, so it can be a little overwhelming at first- knowing how to search it vital. You can search patterns, yarns, and projects. For our purposes I will explain how to search patterns. The first thing you have to do is click on 'search' and then 'patterns'. After that scroll down the side bar and under 'craft' select 'knitting.' Then you can begin browsing patterns!

Ravelry Advanced Search

The filters Ravelry has to offer are what make it such a popular website for patterns. Once you have selected your craft, you can select yarn weight, price of the pattern, construction methods, and more. In the same side bar where you filtered your craft is where you make these selections. You can even filter difficulty levels.

How to read a pattern

Before you start using a pattern, it's very important that you know how to read one. The pattern will have a legend at the top explaining what the abbreviations mean. A pattern will be structured by rows, one row means the total number of your stitches have been knit.
