Frequently Asked Questions

Knitting can be pretty overwhelming, and there are a lot of common mistakes even experienced knitters can make!

My knit stitches look more like Y's than V's, why is that?

That would be because the stitches are twisted. Whether you're using a purl or knit stitch, you should always wrap your yarn around the needle counter clockwise.

I just realized I dropped a stitch, do I have to start over now?

No! There's no need to start over, grab a crochet hook and pull the loops up through each row until you've reached your needle.

I frogged my work, can I use the yarn again?

Of course! In most cases yarn can be used over and over and over again. Just make sure you wind all the loose yarn into a ball so it doesn't get tangled.

I finished a project, now what?

Always make sure to block your project. This evens out the stitches and 'sets' the piece in place. After that, revel in your masterpiece!

Where are the best places to buy yarn?

Yarn.com, Knitpicks.com, or your local yarn store. Ravelry.com even has a LYS locater. Hover over Advanced Search, then click on LYS and enter your location or postal code.

How do I know how much yarn I need for a project?

On the first page of your pattern there will be a section indicating how many yards/meters/grams you need. Look on the label of your yarn and it will tell you length and weight in either ounces or grams. Always round up if you're in between a number of skeins.

What do I do with all these loose ends?

The supplies needed to weave in your ends is just a tapestry needle. To weave them in seamlessly, duplicate stitch a few times and cut the excess yarn tail.

My pattern is talking about 'gauge,' what is that?

Gauge refers to the amount of stitches and rows it takes to get a certain amount of inches. I always recommend doing a gauge swatch to make sure your piece will turn out the right size. A gauge swatch usually measures about 4 inches, and the pattern will indicate how many stitches to cast on.
